Introduction

If you are a PAYE taxpayer, that is if you have tax deducted from your income by your employer, you can use the PAYE Anytime service and the Jobs and Pensions service from Revenue.

This service is now available through Revenue's myAccount Service. These services are now available through Revenue's myAccount Service which is a single access point for secure online services (excluding ROS). Revenue’s myAccount Service is for people who are not registered for ROS.

You can change your contact details, including your address, using myAccount.

PAYE Anytime

If you are a PAYE taxpayer who is not registered for ROS, you can use the PAYE Anytime service. Once you have registered, you can:

  • View your tax record
  • Claim a wide range of tax credits
  • Apply for refunds of tax including health expenses
  • Declare additional income
  • Request a review of tax liability for previous years
  • Re-allocate credits between yourself and your spouse or civil partner
  • Track correspondence submitted to Revenue

PAYE Anytime is available to customers who merely want access to their tax details. You do not have to submit transactions electronically if you do not wish to.

You do not have to submit paper claims when you submit a transaction through PAYE Anytime. However you are obliged to retain receipts for 6 years as Revenue may ask to view them at a later stage.

When a transaction is submitted using PAYE Anytime, it will be immediately acknowledged and a permanent record of the transaction will be put in Your Requests History.

PAYE Anytime is available for the current and previous 4 years.

You can read detailed FAQs on the PAYE Anytime service on Revenue's website.

Jobs and Pensions

If you are starting work, the Jobs and Pensions online service allows you to register your new job with Revenue. It is also available in ROS. It is for people who are:

  • Starting their first job in the current tax year (including their first ever job in Ireland
  • Changing job and their previous job is no longer on Revenue records
  • Starting another in addition to their main job
  • Starting to receive payments from a private pension (that is, not a Department of Social Protection pension)

When you have registered the details of your new job or private pension, Revenue will send your employer a tax credit certificate showing the tax credits that your employer deducts from your tax bill.

How to apply

To access the PAYE Anytime service you must register with Revenue's myAccount Service.

Detailed FAQs on myAccount are available on Revenue’s website.

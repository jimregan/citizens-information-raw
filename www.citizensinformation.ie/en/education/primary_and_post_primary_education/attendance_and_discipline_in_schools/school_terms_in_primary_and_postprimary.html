School terms in primary and post-primary schools

  • Information
  • Rules

Information

Schools in Ireland are obliged to open for 183 days per year at primary level and 167 days per year at post-primary level. At one time, schools had a good deal of flexibility about the start and end of school terms. This meant that there was considerable variation about when students returned to school after holidays such as Christmas and mid-term breaks.

Since 2004 school terms have been standardised under a series of agreements made between representatives of schools, teacher unions, parentsâ€™ organisations and other relevant bodies.

The dates for the start and the end of the school year are not standardised so schools have some discretion about when students finish school in the summer and when they start school in the autumn. All schools are closed for July and most (or all) of August. The dates for the Christmas, Easter and mid-term breaks are standardised.

At the start of the school year, parents/guardians will get a list of days that the school will be closed during the year, including holidays and training days for teachers. If there are other unexpected closures the school will let you know in advance.

The Department of Education and Skills Circular 0016/2014 deals with the standardisation of the school year 2016/2017. Circular 0009/2017 deals with the standardisation of the school years from 2017/2018 to 2019/2020. The circulars also provides guidance about how schools can make up any time that is lost due to unforeseen closures.

Rules

Start and end of the school year

The dates for the start and the end of the school year at both primary and post-primary level are not fixed. However, due to the start of certificate exams, post-primary schools are usually not open for tuition after the Friday before the June public holiday (the first Monday in June) in any year. The school year generally starts in the week in which 1 September falls .

Discretionary days

Every school must be open for tuition for a minimum of 183 days at primary level and 167 days at post-primary level. Schools can use any remaining days at their discretion to extend the summer holiday period or to close on religious or other holidays.

However, schools cannot use these days to extend the Christmas, Easter or mid-term breaks (unless a religious observance day(s) falls at that time for schools of a particular denomination or faith). If a school does not have enough discretionary days left for a religious holiday, it can denote that day a non-tuition day.

School year 2016/17

October 2016 mid-term break

All schools close from Monday 31 October 2016 to Friday 4 November 2016 inclusive.

Christmas 2016

All schools close on Thursday 22 December 2016, which is the final day of the school term. All schools re-open on Monday 9 January 2017.

February 2017 mid-term break

Primary schools close for 2 days, on Thursday 23 February 2017 and Friday 24 February 2017. They may use 3 discretionary days to extend this to a 5-day break starting on Monday 20 February, unless they need these days to make up for time lost due to unforeseen school closures.

Post-primary schools close for a week, from Monday 20 February 2017 to Friday 24 February 2017 inclusive, unless changes are needed to make up for time lost due to unforeseen school closures.

If a post-primary school needs to make up such time, it may shorten the mid-term break by staying open up to and including Wednesday 22 February 2017.

Easter 2017

All schools close on Friday 7 April 2017, which is the final day of the school term, unless changes are needed to make up for time lost due to unforeseen school closures. If a school needs to make up such time, it may shorten the mid-term break by staying open up to and including Wednesday 12 April 2017.

All schools re-open on Monday 24 April 2017.

School year 2017/18

October 2017 mid-term break

All schools close from Monday 30 October 2017 to Friday 3 November 2017 inclusive.

Christmas 2017

All schools close on Friday 22 December 2017, which is the final day of the school term. All schools re-open on Monday 8 January 2018.

February 2018 mid-term break

Primary schools close for 2 days, on Thursday 15 February 2018 and Friday 16 February 2018. They may use 3 discretionary days to extend this to a 5-day break starting on Monday 12 February, unless they need these days to make up for time lost due to unforeseen school closures.

Post-primary schools close for a week, from Monday 12 February 2018 to Friday 16 February 2018 inclusive, unless changes are needed to make up for time lost due to unforeseen school closures.

If a post-primary school needs to make up such time, it may shorten the mid-term break by staying open up to and including Wednesday 14 February 2018.

Easter 2018

All schools close on Friday 23 March 2018, which is the final day of the school term, unless changes are needed to make up for time lost due to unforeseen school closures. If a school needs to make up such time, it may shorten the mid-term break by staying open up to and including Wednesday 28 March 2018.

All schools re-open on Monday 9 April 2018.
